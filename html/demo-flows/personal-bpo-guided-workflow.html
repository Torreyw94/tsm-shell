What BPO means in your world

BPO means you help a business take messy operational work — documents, queues, backlogs, approvals, exceptions, and reports — and turn it into a managed process with visibility, ownership, and next actions.

Your job is not to be the expert in every industry

  • You are the workflow operator.
  • You organize intake.
  • You identify where work gets stuck.
  • You use TSM Neural Core to summarize risk and next actions.
  • You help the client see what needs attention first.

Never position yourself as

  • A doctor
  • A lawyer
  • A CPA
  • A licensed contractor
  • A final decision-maker

Position yourself as the operational intelligence / workflow support layer.

Say this to yourself

I am not replacing the client’s professionals.
I am helping them see their queue, documents, blockers, risks, owners, and next actions faster.

Discovery call goal

The goal of the first call is not to solve everything. The goal is to find one painful workflow that can be improved in 30 days.

Ask these questions

  • What work is currently stuck or delayed?
  • What documents are reviewed manually?
  • What queue causes the most stress?
  • Where do mistakes cost money?
  • Who owns the work today?
  • How do you know something is overdue?
  • What report do executives ask for repeatedly?

Listen for these signals

  • “We track that in spreadsheets.”
  • “We email back and forth.”
  • “Nobody knows until month-end.”
  • “It depends who catches it.”
  • “We do that manually.”
  • “We need better visibility.”

Good discovery close

Based on what you described, I would not start with everything.
I would start with one queue, one document type, and one measurable workflow.
Then we prove whether TSM can reduce manual review, improve visibility, and accelerate escalation.

Document intake rule

Every document should answer one question: what decision, risk, delay, or next action does this document help reveal?

Healthcare intake examples

  • Referral packet
  • Prior authorization request
  • Payer denial letter
  • Eligibility notes
  • Claim queue export

FinOps intake examples

  • Invoice summary
  • GL extract
  • Bank recon
  • AP aging
  • Close checklist

Construction intake examples

  • Change order
  • RFI
  • Submittal
  • Progress report
  • Invoice / pay app
  • Incident report

Legal / Tax / RRD intake examples

  • Matter intake notes
  • Contract sample
  • Billing audit export
  • Tax organizer
  • IRS notice
  • Receivable payment history

Before uploading, ask

What is this document?
Who owns the next action?
What happens if this is delayed?
What evidence is missing?
What decision should this support?

How to read a BNCA output

Do not treat the BNCA as magic. Treat it as a structured first-pass operating recommendation that needs human review.

Look for these fields

  • Top issue
  • Risk level
  • Best next actions
  • Owner lane
  • SLA pressure
  • Missing evidence
  • Confidence

Your review questions

  • Is the recommendation specific?
  • Does it identify an owner?
  • Does it mention missing evidence?
  • Does it show urgency?
  • Can the client act on this today?

How to explain BNCA to clients

BNCA means Best Next Course of Action.
It does not replace your judgment.
It helps prioritize what needs to happen next, who should own it, and what evidence supports the decision.

Client reporting rule

Executives do not want every detail. They want what changed, what is stuck, what is risky, who owns it, and what happens next.

Weekly report format

  • Queue volume
  • Items completed
  • Items stuck
  • SLA risks
  • Financial / compliance exposure
  • Top 3 next actions

Never overpromise

  • Do not guarantee recovery.
  • Do not give legal/medical/tax advice.
  • Do not claim the AI is always right.
  • Do not say data is production-secure until controls are in place.

Client-safe wording

Based on the uploaded context, the system flagged this as a priority workflow item.
The recommendation should be reviewed by your responsible team member before final action.

30-day pilot structure

A strong pilot is small, measurable, and focused. Do not sell the whole platform first. Sell one painful workflow.

Pilot setup

  • Choose one department.
  • Choose one queue.
  • Choose 2–3 document types.
  • Define success metrics.
  • Run weekly reviews.
  • Deliver executive rollup.

Success metrics

  • Reduced manual review time
  • Faster escalation
  • Fewer missed items
  • Improved queue visibility
  • Earlier risk detection
  • Cleaner executive reporting

Your pilot ask

I recommend we start with a 30-day proof of concept.
We will choose one workflow, define the documents, track the queue, run BNCA review, and deliver a weekly executive summary.
At the end, we decide whether the operational improvement is worth expanding.

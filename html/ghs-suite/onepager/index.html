What Banner Sees in the Pilot

  • One workflow first: prior authorization or denials.
  • Real documents: processed through healthcare nodes and strategist logic.
  • Action-ready output: BNCA, letters, compliance notes, and manager routing.
  • Leadership view: operational posture and measurable impact from the executive portal.

Why This Does Not Replace Epic / Cerner

TSM Healthcare Command is not positioned as a replacement EHR. It is an intelligence and action layer above existing systems. Epic or Cerner remains the system of record. TSM helps teams decide what to do next, generate supporting outputs, and surface operational risk to managers and executives.

90-Day Pilot Structure

  • Weeks 1–2: define one workflow, sample documents, success metrics, and pilot users.
  • Weeks 3–8: live execution across documents, HC nodes, BNCA, and generated follow-up outputs.
  • Weeks 9–12: validate time saved, risk reduced, and revenue protected; prepare expansion recommendation.
“This is not another dashboard. It is a decision layer that tells the office what to do next — and shows leadership the operational impact.”

Recommended Starting Point

Start with denials or prior authorization as the first 90-day pilot workflow. These areas create measurable impact quickly: faster decision cycles, fewer preventable delays, better documentation, and clearer manager escalation.

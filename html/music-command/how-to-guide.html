01 🧬 Foundation

Build Your Artist DNA First

This is the most important step. Your DNA profile is what separates generic AI lyrics from lyrics that actually sound like you. Every agent reads your DNA before generating anything. The more detailed it is, the better everything else works.

1
Go to the Artist DNA Tab
This is your command center for teaching the AI who you are
▼

Click Artist DNA in the top nav or left sidebar. This is separate from all the other tools — it's the foundation everything is built on. Set this up first, before you draft or generate anything.

Pro Move
Think of Artist DNA as the difference between asking a stranger to write for you vs. asking your writing partner who's known you for years. The more you put in here, the less editing you'll need to do on outputs.
2
Add Your Signature Ad-libs & Phrases
The verbal tics, calls, and sounds that are uniquely yours
▼

Type each one and hit Enter or the + button. These are the things you say naturally — the fillers, punctuation, energy markers. They get placed automatically in the right spots when you generate or revise lyrics.

Examples of ad-libs to add: yeah!, ay, uh, let's go, run it, okay, on sight (laughs), *whistles*, skrrt, ayy, aye aye [your name], talk to 'em, facts, period, no cap
Tip
Add 8–15 ad-libs for best results. Include your name shouts, crowd hype phrases, and the stuff your fans already recognize and say back to you.
3
Load Your Vocabulary & Slang
The specific words, phrases, and expressions native to your voice
▼

These are the words you reach for naturally. Not general slang — your slang. The terms you've coined, the phrases you repeat, the references your core audience instantly recognizes.

Add things like: "weight to gold" — your signature phrase "the pressure" — your go-to theme word "locked in" — how you describe focus "on the come-up" — your narrative word City-specific slang, neighborhood references, culture words
4
Define Your Flow Patterns
How you actually deliver bars — the rhythmic blueprint
▼

This shapes the cadence and syllable patterns the agents aim for when writing your lines. Be specific — general answers like "fast" don't help much.

Describe your flow like this: "triplets on beat 3, rest on beat 4" "punch on the last syllable of every bar" "syncopated, off-the-grid delivery" "slow build verse, explosive hook" "double-time bridges, half-time verses" "melodic hooks, rapped verses"
How to figure this out
Listen to your 3 best songs. Clap along. Where do your words land? Where do you breathe? Where do you hold notes? Write that down and add it here.
5
Write Your Artist Bio & Style Notes
The most powerful field in the whole system — don't skip it
▼

Write 3–5 sentences like you're briefing your best ghostwriter. Cover: where you're from, what you write about, your emotional range, what you refuse to sound like, and what makes a lyric feel authentic to you.

Strong bio example: Phoenix-based artist. Dark introspective verses that build to triumphant hooks — the journey from struggle to elevation is my core story. Influenced by late-night city energy, loyalty, and the grind. Voice is raw but melodic. I never write anything I haven't lived. My fans know the real me through my lyrics — I never perform a character. The pressure and the city made me who I am, and that shows up in everything.
What NOT to write
Don't write "I make hip-hop music" or "I like trap beats." That tells the AI nothing. Be specific about your emotional world, your narrative, your red lines.
6
Hit "Save DNA Profile" — Then "Analyze My Style"
Lock it in and see what the AI has learned about you
▼

Save first, then run the Analyze button. The AI will reflect back a creative profile of you — what makes you distinct, your strengths, your signature elements. Read it carefully. If something feels off, update your bio and re-run. The DNA Match % in the sidebar shows how well your profile is calibrated.

Update regularly
Your DNA profile should grow over time. Every time you write a new song, add new vocab, new motifs, new phrases that came out naturally. Treat it like a living document.
02 📁 DNA Training

Can You Upload Songs & Instrumentals?

Short answer: yes — but the method matters depending on what you're uploading. Here's exactly how to use your existing music catalog to teach the AI your DNA.

✅ Yes — with the right approach for each file type
The AI works with lyrics, text, and audio transcripts directly. Instrumentals (MP3/WAV) need a quick workaround, but your written songs, voice memos, and any text-based lyrics work immediately. Here's how to handle each type:
📝 Completed Song Lyrics
Songs you've already written — your strongest DNA source
  • Copy the full lyrics of 3–5 of your best songs
  • Paste them into the Artist Bio field — write "Reference lyrics:" above each one
  • Or paste one song at a time into the Draft field and run "Analyze My Style" to extract vocab and patterns
  • Manually add standout phrases, hooks, and ad-libs into their respective DNA fields
  • Run "Analyze My Style" — the AI will read your references and build a profile
🎵 Instrumentals & Beat Demos
MP3/WAV files — use this workaround to extract style info
  • Upload the instrumental to a transcription tool (like Suno, Udio, or any DAW)
  • Describe the beat's energy, tempo, vibe, and emotional feel in words
  • Add that description to your Flow Patterns or Artist Bio field
  • Example: "Most beats are dark trap, 140bpm, minor keys, heavy 808s with melodic samples"
  • The AI will use this to match lyric pacing and syllable density to your production style
🎤 Voice Memos & Freestyles
Rough recordings and freestyle sessions — gold for DNA
  • Transcribe your voice memo using your phone's voice notes or a free tool like Otter.ai
  • Even rough, half-formed bars reveal your natural flow and vocabulary
  • Paste the transcript into Draft + Analysis and request "Extract my natural voice patterns"
  • Take the natural phrases and ad-libs that appear and add them to DNA fields
  • Voice memos often reveal your real voice better than polished songs
📂 Notebooks & Written Drafts
Physical or digital notes, old verses, bars you've saved
  • Photograph notebook pages and use Google Lens or iPhone Photos to extract the text
  • Copy old drafts from Notes, Voice Memos text, or WhatsApp voice note transcripts
  • Paste into Draft + Analysis with the request: "Don't revise — just analyze my style patterns"
  • Pull out the recurring words and metaphors into your Vocabulary and Themes DNA fields
  • The more historical material you feed in, the deeper the AI understands your evolution
🔮 Power Move — Feed 5 Songs, Get Back Your Fingerprint
Paste the lyrics from your 5 strongest songs into the Artist Bio field (under "Reference catalog:"). Then run "Analyze My Style." The AI will extract a creative fingerprint showing your recurring phrases, dominant themes, structural habits, and emotional range. This becomes the blueprint for every future song.
⚠ What Instrumentals Can't Do (Yet)
The system can't directly listen to an MP3 and extract musical information from the audio file itself — that requires audio analysis software like a DAW or audio AI. But it can use your written descriptions of your production style to match lyric pacing, syllable density, energy arcs, and tonal range. Describe your sound in words and it works just as well.

04 🎵 Song Building

Song Structure — Section by Section

Every section of a song has a job. Understanding those jobs is what lets you write each piece intentionally — and tell the AI exactly what you need from each one.

Intro
Set the world — 4 to 8 bars
Establishes the vibe, tempo, and emotional tone. Can be spoken, melodic, or atmospheric. Listeners decide in the first 8 seconds if they stay.
Verse 1
Build the story — 8 to 16 bars
Tells the beginning of the narrative. Sets up the emotional problem or situation. Should be specific — details and imagery, not abstract statements. Ends building toward the pre-hook.
Pre-Hook
Earn the hook — 2 to 4 bars
The transition that makes the hook feel inevitable. Shifts energy, melody, or perspective. Without a strong pre-hook, hooks often feel dropped in instead of earned.
Hook / Chorus
The core statement — 4 to 8 bars
The most memorable, repeatable part of the song. Must work on its own — someone should be able to hum it without knowing anything else. Emotionally resolves what the verse set up. Short sentences, big feeling.
Verse 2
Deepen the story — 8 to 16 bars
Should escalate, not repeat. New details, deeper perspective, or a plot development. Many songs fail here because Verse 2 says the same thing as Verse 1 with different words.
Bridge
The turn — 4 to 8 bars
Shifts the song's perspective or energy. Often the most emotionally raw section. Can be the vulnerability before the final resolution, or the climax of the narrative. Should feel different from verses and hook.
Outro
Land the plane — 4 to 8 bars
Brings the song home. Can echo the intro, resolve the story, or leave with a final statement. Should feel like a conclusion, not just a fade-out. Your last line is what people remember leaving.
🎯 The Structure Command
In the Generate tab, select "Full Song Skeleton" and describe your concept. The AI will map out every section — intro through outro — with notes on what each part should accomplish emotionally and narratively. Use this before you write a single word of the actual song.
05 📝 Draft Mode

Draft + Analysis — Step by Step

Don't just paste and hope. The more specific your request, the better the output. Here's the exact process for getting clean, useful passes every time.

1
Paste ONE section at a time
Don't paste a full 50-bar song — isolate what needs work
▼

The hint at the top of the panel says it directly: "One focused pass at a time gives cleaner results." Paste just the verse, just the hook, or just the bridge that's giving you trouble. The AI gives sharper feedback on a single section than on a wall of text.

Rule of thumb
Paste 4–16 bars max per pass. If you have more, do separate passes.
2
Write a sharp, specific request
The Focused Request field is the most important field on the page
▼

Vague requests get vague results. Instead of "make this better," describe the specific transformation you want. Use the Quick Prompts to start, then customize.

Weak request: "Fix this verse" Strong requests: "Make this darker in the first 4 bars, then have it build into something triumphant by the end" "Tighten every line to match 12 syllables. Keep the same imagery but cut the filler words." "This verse starts too positive — I want it to feel like I'm at rock bottom before the hook saves it"
3
Choose the right agent and priority
These two dropdowns change everything about the output
▼

Match the agent to what's broken. Match the priority to where the song will live.

PriorityWhat it optimizes for
CatalogTimeless quality, depth, replay — for records that last
Live PerformanceCrowd interaction, energy, call-and-response, breathing room
Radio / StreamingHook placement, song length, mainstream accessibility
Underground / NicheAuthenticity, lyricism, detail over polish
Feature VerseStand-out moment, contrast from host artist, bar-for-bar quality
4
Read all 3 options — don't just take Option 1
The 3 options are intentionally different angles on the same request
▼

Each option explores a different interpretation of your request. Often the best solution is Option 2's first two lines combined with Option 3's ending. Read them like a menu — then mix and match in Revision Mode.

Surgeon's approach
Take the best line from each option. Send your assembled version to Revision Mode to polish the seams.
06 ✂ Revision

Revision Mode — The Precision Tool

Revision Mode is the most powerful feature in the system. It gives the AI surgical instructions instead of hoping a vague prompt produces what you want. The 4-box format is intentional — fill all 4 for the best results.

①
Box 1 — Current Draft
Paste exactly what you want revised — nothing extra
▼

This is the source material. Paste only the section you're revising — not a full song, not context. Just the bars. Clean copy, no annotations.

②
Box 2 — Edit Instructions
Tell the AI what specifically needs to change — not just "make it better"
▼

This is where most people underperform. Be a director, not a cheerleader. Tell the AI exactly what's wrong and what the fix should accomplish.

Strong edit instructions: "The first 2 bars are too soft — make them feel like I just walked into the room and owned it immediately. Keep the rhyme scheme (ABAB) but make every line land harder. Cut any word over 3 syllables in bar 1-2." "The hook is 3 bars when it should be 4. Add a 4th bar that echoes the first line but flips the meaning. Tighten the melody — fewer words, more space."
③
Box 3 — What to Protect
Lock in the lines, phrases, or elements that are NOT up for debate
▼

This is what most people forget — and then get frustrated when the AI changes something perfect. List specific lines, phrases, or structural elements that must stay exactly as they are.

Protect examples: "Keep: 'weight to gold' in bar 3 — exact wording" "Keep the AABB rhyme scheme throughout" "Keep: the city reference in line 1" "The last bar of the hook must not change" "Keep my signature pause after bar 6"
④
Box 4 — Output Options You Want Back
Tell the AI the 3 different approaches you want to see
▼

Instead of getting 3 random variations, tell the AI what each option should prioritize. This turns the output from a lottery into a menu of deliberate creative choices.

Option requests example: Option 1: grimy and street — maximum rawness, no polish Option 2: emotional trap — melodic, vulnerability showing Option 3: live performance — crowd energy, call-and-response
Why this matters
Without Box 4, the AI picks 3 angles you didn't ask for. With Box 4, every option serves a specific direction you can actually use.

08 ⚡ Full Process

Complete Song Workflow — Start to Finish

This is the exact sequence to build a full, polished song using every part of the system. Follow this order and you'll spend less time revising and more time recording.

🧬
Step 1 — Set / Update Your DNA Profile
Before touching the song, make sure your Artist DNA is current. Add anything new from recent sessions — new slang, new themes, new ad-libs. Fresh DNA → better output.
🗂
Step 2 — Generate a Full Song Skeleton (Generate Tab)
Use "Full Song Skeleton" + your concept to map every section. This gives you a writing blueprint: what each section does, how it connects, what the emotional arc is. Don't skip this — it saves hours.
🪝
Step 3 — Build the Hook First (Generate → Hook Options)
The hook is the spine of the song. Write verses around it, not the other way around. Generate 3 hook options, pick the strongest, refine in Revision Mode. Save to bank.
📝
Step 4 — Write or Generate Verse 1 (Draft + Analysis)
Either write it yourself and paste into Draft, or generate from concept. Run an agent pass. The goal: verse 1 should set up the exact emotional problem the hook resolves.
✂
Step 5 — Revise Verse 1 to Perfection (Revision Mode)
Use the 4-box system. Protect what's working. Tell it exactly what's wrong. Get 3 options. Combine the best lines. Do 2–3 passes max — don't over-revise. Save to bank.
🔁
Step 6 — Repeat for Verse 2 + Pre-hook + Bridge
Use the same Draft → Revise process for each section. Verse 2 should escalate. Bridge should shift the emotional register. Save everything to bank as you go.
🎤
Step 7 — Place Ad-libs (Studio Tools → Ad-lib Placer)
After all sections are done, paste the full song into the Ad-lib Placer tool. It reads your DNA ad-libs and places them at the rhythmically and emotionally right moments.
✅
Step 8 — Final Pass (Draft + Analysis → Full Command Mode)
Paste the full song. Run "Full Command Mode" with the request: "Read this as a complete song — flag anything that feels inconsistent, weak, or off-voice." This catches the seams between sections.
09 📂 Song Bank

Song Bank — Your Creative Archive

The Song Bank is where nothing gets lost. Every output, draft, and idea you save lives here. Treat it like a producer treats a sample folder.

→
Save Everything — Even What You Don't Use
The line you cut from this song might be the hook of the next one
▼

Hit 💾 Save to Bank on every output you run — even the ones you reject. "Option 2: emotional trap" that didn't fit the current song might be exactly what you need in 3 weeks. Label everything clearly with the title field so you can find it fast.

Good bank labels: "Hook v2 — Pressure Into Gold — darker direction" "Verse 1 alt — more vulnerable version" "Bridge option — before I added the piano reference" "Ad-lib sheet — City Heat song"
→
Use "Load" to Pick Up Where You Left Off
Load any saved output back into Draft or Revision Mode in one click
▼

Click Load on any bank item and it drops directly into the Draft field — ready for another pass, revision, or to send to Generate. The bank makes every past session available to the current one.

→
Add Notes Manually for New Ideas
Voice memo transcript, notebook idea, or stray concept — save it here
▼

Use the "Add Note / Save Current Draft Manually" section at the bottom of Song Bank to paste anything — a rough idea, a phone note, a reference lyric. This becomes your idea pool that feeds future sessions.

11🔁 Iteration Loop

The Real Workflow — Pick → Rerun → Improve

Generate options, choose the strongest one, then run it back through ZAY, RIYA, and DJ. The selected version becomes the new working draft and the system tracks improvement.

Step 1 — Generate 3 revision options
The system creates Flow First, Emotion First, and Hook First paths.
Step 2 — Review scores
Check cadence, emotion, structure, imagery, and overall score.
Step 3 — Pick + Run Again
Your selected option reruns through the multi-agent chain and stores learning into Artist DNA.
Step 4 — Track evolution
Use the timeline to see whether the song improves, stalls, or needs a new hook direction.

10 ⚡ Pro Level

Pro Tips — Separating Good from Elite

The difference between artists who get average outputs and artists who get outputs they actually record isn't the tool — it's how they use it.

💡 Elite Habits Checklist
Update your DNA profile after every session — new phrases, new themes, new flows
Always build the hook first — then write verses to set it up, not the other way around
Use Box 3 (Protect) in Revision Mode every single time — never let the AI change what's already perfect
Combine lines from different options instead of taking one option wholesale
Paste your top 5 songs as DNA reference — your best work teaches the AI your ceiling
Do 2–3 revision passes max — over-revising makes lyrics feel sterile
Use the Syllable Counter before recording to find lines that'll fight the beat
Run the Metaphor Lab when a theme feels stale — 15 fresh metaphors breaks creative blocks fast
Label Song Bank saves clearly — you'll thank yourself in 3 months
Run a "Full Command Mode" final pass on every completed song before recording
Trust your voice over the AI — use it as a collaborator, not a ghostwriter
🎤 The Golden Rule
The AI knows craft. You know your truth. The best lyrics come from combining both — let the AI handle syllable counts, rhyme schemes, and structure while you supply the lived experience, the specific details, and the emotional authenticity that no AI can manufacture. Give it your raw material. It gives back the polished version. Then you decide what feels real.

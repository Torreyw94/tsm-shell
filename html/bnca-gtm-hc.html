Approach 2

Demo script for closing deals

Start with their pain, run BNCA, read the output, tie it to money, and close toward a focused pilot.
1
Open strong
start with a hard question
Ask
Say: "What's the biggest issue your office is dealing with right now — claims backlog, prior auth delays, billing lag, compliance pressure, or staffing drag?"
This creates relevance immediately. Do not begin with a product tour.
2
Frame the system
explain BNCA in one sentence
Frame
Say: "Instead of making your team dig through systems and meetings to decide what to do next, BNCA tells your office the best next course of action in under a minute."
Keep it simple and outcome-focused.
3
Run the analysis
use a live scenario
Prompt
Example live prompt: "Review denial pressure, prior auth delays, compliance drift, staffing throughput, and site-level blockers. Return the Best Next Course of Action for the office manager."
This shows operational intelligence, not generic AI output.
4
Show the result
read the BNCA output out loud
Proof
Say: "Here is the top issue. Here is why it matters. Here is the best next action. Here are the owner lanes. Here is what should happen in the next 24 to 72 hours."
The output is your proof.
5
Tie to money
translate action into value
Value
Say: "What matters here is not the dashboard. It's that your office now knows where to move first, where money is getting stuck, and which lane is responsible."
Buyers care about recovered revenue, fewer delays, and less chaos.
6
Close
offer the pilot
Next step
Say: "The easiest next step is a focused BNCA pilot in one office or one operating lane. We can get you live fast and start showing your team what to do next right away."
Do not end with “Any questions?” End with a clear next step.

Study mode

Presentation notes for Thursday

These are for you, not the prospect. Practice the logic, not every line.
What to emphasize
  • Lead with pain and clarity, not architecture.
  • Say “Best Next Course of Action” often enough that it becomes the mental frame.
  • Translate every screen into an operational outcome.
  • When showing the suite, tie each app to a lane: claims, denials, compliance, staffing, vendors, tax, grants.
  • Always explain who should act next, not just what the data says.
How to talk about the healthcare suite
your mental model
Narrative
Simple story: 1. Offices are overloaded. 2. Critical issues are spread across billing, claims, compliance, staffing, and vendor workflows. 3. Teams lose time deciding what matters most. 4. BNCA turns that chaos into one next action path. 5. Your healthcare suite is the operating intelligence layer that makes that possible. Best single sentence: "We tell your office what to do next." Best second sentence: "Then we show exactly which lane should move first and why."
30-second version
talk track
Short form
"Healthcare teams don't have a visibility problem as much as they have a decision problem. Our BNCA layer sits across billing, claims, compliance, staffing, and operations and tells the office what to do next, which lane owns it, and what should happen in the next 24 to 72 hours."
60-second version
talk track
Expanded
"Most offices are juggling too many systems and too much noise. Claims get stuck, prior auths drag, billing lag grows, and compliance issues hide in the background until they become expensive. We built BNCA to solve that. Instead of asking teams to figure out what matters most, BNCA gives the office the best next course of action in under a minute. That means faster decisions, cleaner accountability, and a clearer path to recovered revenue and lower operational drag."
Study tip: do not memorize every word. Memorize the structure. Pain → BNCA promise → proof output → business value → pilot next step.

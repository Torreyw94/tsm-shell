What TSM provides Honor Health

Dee's command portal

Dee's TSM Honor Portal is the single source of truth across every Honor Health satellite office in the Phoenix metro. Instead of receiving status updates through emails, phone calls, or meetings, Dee sees a live dashboard — every office manager's node activity, every delegation, every BNCA recommendation surfaced by that office's HC-Strategist — all in one view, in real time.

What office managers gain

Each office manager at a satellite location gets their own TSM portal at health.tsmatter.com. That portal houses their office's 10 HC-Nodes and their dedicated HC-Strategist — the intelligence engine that ingests their office's operational data and surfaces BNCA (Best Next Course of Action) recommendations. The manager sees exactly what needs attention, acts on it, and delegates each HC-node responsibility to the right frontline worker — all within the portal, all tracked automatically.

What frontline workers gain

Rather than receiving informal task lists or verbal assignments, each frontline worker is formally delegated responsibility for one or more HC-nodes. Their node is their accountability — they own it in the system. Their activity on that node feeds back into the office's data, which the HC-Strategist continuously analyzes to generate the next round of BNCA recommendations for their manager. The loop is complete, automated, and transparent at every level.

Why this transforms the current workflow

Honor Health's current workflow depends on individual initiative, informal communication, and manual reporting. TSM replaces that with a structured, role-aware system where accountability is built in — not bolted on. Every change is visible to the level above it. Every recommendation is grounded in real office data. And because the portal scales identically across all Phoenix locations, Dee doesn't manage five different operational styles — she manages one system, applied consistently everywhere.

The HC-Strategist · intelligence layer explained

What the HC-Strategist actually does

The HC-Strategist is not a dashboard. It is an analytical engine embedded in each office manager's portal that continuously ingests the live operational data flowing through that office's 10 HC-nodes — volume, velocity, exceptions, backlogs, expiration timelines, and throughput patterns. From that data, it generates BNCA (Best Next Course of Action) recommendations specific to that office's current operational state.

For example: if HC-06 (Credentialing) shows three provider licenses expiring within 45 days and no renewal submissions started, the HC-Strategist surfaces a BNCA recommendation to the office manager to delegate credentialing renewal immediately — before it becomes a compliance problem. The manager doesn't need to know to look. The system tells them.

Why BNCA recommendations change how managers operate

Most office managers today operate reactively — they respond to problems that have already surfaced. The HC-Strategist shifts that to proactive management. Because the system is always analyzing node data, the manager is always looking one step ahead. And because the recommendations are grounded in that office's actual data — not generic best practices — they're actionable immediately. The manager reads the recommendation, reviews their nodes, and delegates. The loop closes in the same session.

How BPO and the portal connect

BPO services don't operate separately from the portal — they operate through it. When an office manager delegates a node function to TSM rather than an internal frontline worker, that delegation is still tracked the same way: in-portal, timestamped, attributable. The intake portal handles document and request submissions. The client dashboard shows processing status. Dee sees BPO activity the same way she sees internal node activity — as part of the same live view. There is no separate BPO tracking system. TSM is the system.
